ECOgnize

Every day, up to 150 species of plants and animals go extinct. Conservationists do not have a way to effectively recognize and compile ecological data from camera traps placed in the wild.

MY ROLE

I was responsible for the research, interaction design, visual design, and front‑end development for ‘ECOgnize’

THE CHALLENGE

Find a more efficient way to identify images taken by camera traps. Currently volunteers sift through thousands of images taken by camera traps to identify any potential poaching activity.

THE APPROACH

Our lack of domain knowledge in animal conservation meant we needed to understand the nature of poaching throughly and quickly. We approached all aspects of the project collaboratively with wild life conservationists and spent most of our time working on‑site at the San Diego Wild Animal Park. Throughout the project we were able to ask questions to the conservationists about their current means of image recognition.
We decided to bring together the Google Vision , Microsoft Azure, and Amazon ReKognition API to create an application that is able to identify images taken by camera traps. Utilizing all three APIs allows for a better chance of recieving images that are correctly and clearly identifed.

REFLECTION

We believed that a streamlined UI that did all of the data recognition behind the scenes would make for a efficient and effortless experience when trying to catch poachers. However, most animal conservationists are scientists and all scientists value seeing data. A redesign of ECOgnize would include all of the images gathered and the stats of their accuracy taken from each API. The images with the highest accuracy would be the first images displayed.
